A new shower door can give your ordinary bathroom luxurious, spa-like atmosphere - but there's more to picking the right door for your space than finding one that suits your style. Shower doors are available in a huge range of styles, including complete shower enclosures, tub doors, steam shower doors, and shower screens. You'll find a wide array of swings and slides, as well as a variety of glass textures, hardware, and even colors, so here's what you need to know to make the right choice.

Shower Doors

Shower doors come in several styles, including sliding, hinged and bi-fold. The type of shower setup you choose, as well as the size of your tub or stand-alone shower and whether it's in an alcove or corner, will inform your decision on what's right for your bathroom.

Shower Door Styles

The most common shower doors sit on a track and slide back and forth, but there are several other styles available.

• Hinged shower doors. Hinged doors open and close like all the other doors in your home. They work best in bathrooms with plenty of space for a door to open.

• Pivot shower doors. Pivot doors swing but rest on a floor-mounted hinge that allows them to rotate 180 degrees. These modern bathroom add-ons can also be mounted in the center of your shower entrance for a complete 360-degree rotation.

• Sliding shower doors. Sliding doors are the most common, and they sit on tracks to allow you to move either side - or both at the same time.

• Single sliding shower doors. These doors feature one fixed door and one that slides open and closed. You can choose which direction is right for your home.

• Bi-fold shower doors. Bi-fold shower doors have two glass panels. They operate on hinges, and they're perfect for small bathrooms because they don't require a lot of clearance.

• Shower screens. Shower screens are fixed, single glass panels - they don't open or close. They're used in open, luxury showers that have a raised edge to prevent water from flowing to the rest of the floor or that feature recessed drains in the center of a slightly sloped floor.

Shower Enclosures

Shower enclosures are used in corners rather than in alcoves. They typically provide three sides of coverage (or 90-degree circular coverage) and can feature hinged, sliding or folding doors. The number of glass panels varies, and you can choose other variables like frame finishes, glass texture, and more.

Tub Doors

Tub doors are designed to attach to a full-sized bathtub. You can choose sliding doors, hinged doors and other styles.

Steam Shower Doors

Steam shower doors are made to hold in moisture from the base to the ceiling. Typically, these doors feature an additional glass panel at the top - above the doors - as well as air-tight hinges and seals. Most have transoms (movable panels) that let you vent out the shower when you need fresh air.

How to Choose a Shower Door  

Choosing your shower door has a lot to do with the type of tub you have or whether you have a stand-alone shower. When you zero in on the type of door you need, you'll be able to choose based on:

• Glass thickness. Typically, a standard fixed panel is made from 3/8-inch glass. Usually, that width is sturdy enough for frequent home use. However, tall doors are usually made from 1/2-inch-thick glass (especially if they're over 84 inches tall) because that thickness reduces the chance of bowing and is a bit sturdier. Rarely, you'll find doors made from 1/4-inch glass - but it's much more fragile than other thicknesses are.

• Frame finish. The frame finish you choose should match your other bathroom hardware, like faucets and cabinet pulls. You'll find shower door frames in a spectacular array of finishes, including stainless steel and silver tones, brass, and a variety of colors.

• Glass texture. Your options for glass texture include clear, frosted and pattern. If privacy is high on your must-have list, you'll want frosted or patterned glass - but if you're not too worried about it, clear glass provides a spectacularly elegant and polished look.
